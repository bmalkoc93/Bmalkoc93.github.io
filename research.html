Broadly, I research Chinese foreign investments and resource extraction in West Africa.

My master's research used political ecology to understand the complexities facing residents of Jamestown harbor who were the target of ongoing demolitions by the city municipal government to make way for a new Chinese-backed harbor. I conducted two months of qualitative fieldwork, including semi-structured interviews and a participatory drawing method which I designed, to study how residents rebuilt smaller dwellings with less expensive materials. In understanding how peoples' dwellings changed, this research contributes to a deeper understanding of the issues that vulnerbale communities face. Unequal access to resources (especially of fish due to foreign overfishing in West Africa) limited how residents could construct their dwellings, while living in anticipation of future demolitions forced people to make short-term decisions that led to increased precarity.
